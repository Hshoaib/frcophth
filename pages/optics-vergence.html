Vergence

Vergence describes how strongly a bundle of light rays is converging or diverging at a given plane. It's measured in dioptres (D), and it's simply the reciprocal of the distance to the point the rays are heading toward (or coming from), measured in metres.

L = n / l

where L is the vergence in dioptres, n is the refractive index of the medium (1.00 in air), and l is the distance in metres. Converging light has positive vergence; diverging light has negative vergence.

0.50 m+2.00 D
Light converging to a point 0.50 m away has a vergence of +2.00 D.

Build the instinct for the exam: 1 m → 1 D, 0.5 m → 2 D, 0.25 m → 4 D, 0.1 m → 10 D. Halving the distance doubles the vergence.

Quick check

A lens forms an image 25 cm behind it. What is the vergence of light leaving the lens at that image?

l = 0.25 m, so L = 1 / 0.25 = +4.00 D (converging toward a real image).
