Acute coronary syndromes

ACS is a spectrum from unstable angina through to full-thickness infarction, separated by the ECG and troponin.

STEMI

Ischaemic chest pain with persistent ST elevation or new LBBB; troponin rises.

ST elevation

NSTEMI

Ischaemic pain, raised troponin, but no persistent ST elevation (may show ST depression / T-wave changes).

↑ troponin, no ST elevation

Unstable angina

Ischaemic pain at rest or crescendo, normal troponin and no ST elevation.

normal troponin
First-line management
Initial: aspirin 300 mg, and treat hypoxia/pain as needed. STEMI → immediate reperfusion: primary PCI if available within the target window, otherwise fibrinolysis. NSTEMI/UA → risk-stratify (e.g. GRACE) to time angiography and antiplatelet/anticoagulant therapy. This is a revision summary — always follow current local guidelines.
